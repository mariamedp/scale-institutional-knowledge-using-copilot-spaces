# OctoAcme Role Onboarding & Cross-Team Collaboration Checklist

This document provides a structured checklist for onboarding new team members into their roles and fostering effective cross-team collaboration at OctoAcme.

> **Related:** This document was created to address gaps in onboarding and collaboration as part of [issue #4](https://github.com/mariamedp/scale-institutional-knowledge-using-copilot-spaces/issues/4).

---

## Role Onboarding Checklist

Use this checklist when a new team member joins any role. Adapt items as needed for specific roles.

### General Onboarding (All Roles)

- [ ] Complete HR and IT setup (accounts, hardware, access)
- [ ] Review the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md)
- [ ] Review the [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) document
- [ ] Meet with your manager to discuss expectations, goals, and success metrics
- [ ] Identify your onboarding buddy or mentor
- [ ] Join relevant Slack/Teams channels and distribution lists
- [ ] Add key meetings to your calendar (standups, syncs, retrospectives)
- [ ] Review current project boards and backlogs
- [ ] Complete any required compliance or security training

### Role-Specific Onboarding

#### Developers
- [ ] Review the codebase and development environment setup
- [ ] Understand CI/CD pipelines and release processes ([Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md))
- [ ] Review coding standards and contribution guidelines
- [ ] Pair with a senior developer on an initial task
- [ ] Understand how to raise and triage defects with QA

#### QA Engineers/Testers
- [ ] Review existing test plans and automation frameworks
- [ ] Understand the release criteria and quality gates
- [ ] Shadow a QA session and participate in defect triage
- [ ] Meet with Developers to discuss test coverage expectations
- [ ] Review the [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)

#### UX Designers
- [ ] Review design systems, style guides, and design tools in use
- [ ] Understand the handoff process with Developers
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers to review current product roadmap and user research
- [ ] Participate in a design review or critique session
- [ ] Review documentation on accessibility standards

#### Technical Writers
- [ ] Review existing documentation and style guides
- [ ] Understand the documentation publishing workflow
- [ ] Meet with Developers and Product Managers to identify documentation priorities
- [ ] Review the [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) for release notes expectations
- [ ] Connect with Support Engineers to identify documentation gaps

#### Business Analysts
- [ ] Review current requirements documentation and templates
- [ ] Understand the requirements gathering and sign-off process
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers and Project Managers to review ongoing initiatives
- [ ] Shadow a requirements workshop or stakeholder interview
- [ ] Review how requirements are tracked in project boards

#### Product Managers
- [ ] Review the product roadmap and backlog
- [ ] Meet with Developers, QA, and UX to understand current state
- [ ] Understand how features are prioritized and success is measured
- [ ] Review the [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) document
- [ ] Attend a sprint planning and retrospective session

#### Project Managers
- [ ] Review the [Project Initiation Guide](octoacme-project-initiation.md) and [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)
- [ ] Understand risk management and escalation paths ([Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md))
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers and Developers to understand current projects
- [ ] Review project boards and status reporting processes
- [ ] Attend a stakeholder update meeting

#### Support Engineers
- [ ] Review the knowledge base and FAQ resources
- [ ] Understand the ticket triage and escalation process
- [ ] Meet with Developers to understand common issues and escalation paths
- [ ] Shadow a support shift or incident response session
- [ ] Review the [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) for rollback procedures

## Cross-Team Collaboration Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure effective collaboration across roles and teams.

### Communication & Visibility
- [ ] Ensure all team members have access to shared project boards and documentation
- [ ] Establish and document regular sync meetings (e.g., weekly cross-functional review)
- [ ] Agree on communication channels for quick questions, escalations, and announcements
- [ ] Maintain a shared calendar for key milestones, releases, and reviews

### Handoff Practices
- [ ] Document handoff points in the project workflow (e.g., design to dev, dev to QA)
- [ ] Ensure each handoff includes explicit acceptance criteria and a named owner
- [ ] Review handoff effectiveness in retrospectives and address any recurring gaps

### Collaboration Norms
- [ ] Encourage early and frequent feedback—flag issues as soon as they arise
- [ ] Hold regular cross-functional syncs (e.g., sprint planning, design/dev/QA review)
- [ ] Use shared documentation for requirements, designs, and decisions
- [ ] Celebrate wins and recognize contributions across teams

### Accountability & Quality
- [ ] Each deliverable has a single owner and one or more contributors
- [ ] Track accountability through project boards and status updates
- [ ] Review accountability and quality gaps in retrospectives
- [ ] QA and Support Engineers provide feedback loops for continuous improvement

### Continuous Improvement
- [ ] Capture learnings and action items from retrospectives
- [ ] Regularly review and update process documentation
- [ ] Encourage experimentation and process improvements
- [ ] Share best practices across teams

## Tips for Successful Onboarding & Collaboration

- **Ask questions early:** Don't hesitate to reach out to your buddy, mentor, or teammates.
- **Document as you learn:** Contribute to the knowledge base by noting gaps or improvements.
- **Build relationships:** Meet people across teams—collaboration is easier when you know your colleagues.
- **Embrace feedback:** Give and receive feedback openly to foster a culture of improvement.
- **Stay aligned:** Regularly revisit project goals, roles, and responsibilities to stay on track.
